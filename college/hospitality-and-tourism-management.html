College of Hospitality and Tourism Management

About

The tourism and hospitality industry is one of the world's largest industries and offers unrivalled opportunities for an exciting and rewarding career.

UC College of Hospitality and Tourism Management offers programs with integrated specializations having skills certification embedded and industry-driven curriculum towards the production of globally competitive graduates.

Students enjoy competency- based approached and industry-based facilities equipped with up-to-date learning equipment to execute operational tasks and management functions in food production, accommodation, food and beverage service, product development, tourism planning, events planning, transportation services, travel and tour operations and other emerging sectors of tourism and hospitality industry.

Vision

To be the center of excellence in providing quality training for future hoteliers, restaurateurs and tourism professionals equipped with functional knowledge, skills and right attitude.

Mission

To develop, transform and produce globally competent leaders in the hospitality and tourism industry through sound curricula, industry-based facilities and professional instructions.

Programs

  • Bachelor of Science in Hospitality Management
    • Culinary and Food Service Management
    • Hotel and Restaurant Administration
  • Bachelor of Science in Tourism Management

Milestones

The following are the highlights of events that transpired throughout the years:

2003

  • The University of the Cordilleras’ Hotel, Restaurant and Tourism programs as an adjunct course in the College of Business Administration.

2004

  • UC HRM students won Grand Prize Winner for Bread Display Competition during the 1st Food and Drinks Festival Universal by Robina Corporation and 1st Prize for the Best Original Recipe Cooking Contest by Purefoods-Hormel.

2005

  • UC’s Bachelor of Science in Tourism and Bachelor of Science in Hotel and Restaurant Management garnered government recognition by the Commission on Higher Education (CHED).
  • College of Hotel and Restaurant Management and Tourism (CHRMT) was created.
  • UC CHRMT was awarded Over-all Champion during the 1st Ilocandia Culinary Festival.

2006

  • UC CHRMT won 1st and 2nd Place in the Ikebana Competition and in Ryouri: Japanese Cooking Competition.
  • The college was awarded 2nd Over-all Champion during the 3rd HRT Weekend Competitions
  • UC CHRMT Implemented the Ladderized Curriculum as per E.O 358. The First and only University in CAR who participated.

2009

  • The college earned Level I accreditation by the Philippine Association of Colleges and Universities Commission on Accreditation (PACUCOA) for the BS Hospitality Management and BS Tourism Management programs.
  • The college also established a partnership with the Australian Trade Training College (ATTC)* in North Brisbane under the Technical and Further Education (TAFE) which is based on the Australian Qualifications Framework (AQF) and the Australian Qualifications Training Framework (AQTF).
  • UC CHTM students were crowned Mr. HRAB Centennial and garnered also the 1st and 2nd Runner Up for Mr & Ms. HRAB.

2010

  • ATTCNB program was embedded in the new curriculum of HTM.
  • Student population of the college student reached two thousand and seventy one (2071) which is the highest number of enrolees.

2012

  • UC CHTM also became a certified Technical-Vocational Institution (TVI) delivering five (5) programs embedded in the BSHM and BSTM curriculum.
  • The college commence the deployment of student interns industry partners outside Baguio located in Sagada, Banaue, La Union, Ilocos, Manila, Subic, Clark, Batangas, Tagayatay, Cebu, Bohol, Palawan and Boracay for their On-the-Job Training.

2013

  • UC-CHTM co-organized the 1st Northern Luzon Conference of COHREP-CAR.
  • UC-CHTM won Best Decorated Pavilion during the Session Road in Bloom.

2014

  • The College launched the S.P.O.O.N.S (Serving People, Opening Opportunities, Nurturing the Society) as the official community and extension carrier of the college.
  • Three community outreach programs were conducted. Tsinelas ni Juan project; Pagkain ni Juan project; Sandok kawali project.

2015

  • The college explored opportunities to open short-term programs to address the growing and emerging demand for specialized courses. TESDA granted UC for TWSP Scholarship with slots for Bread and Pastry Production NCII and Housekeeping Services NCII.

2016

  • UC-CHTM won Best Decorated Pavilion during the Session Road in Bloom.

2017

  • Soft opening and operations of the UC-CHTM Tribu Café/Coffee Shop.

2018

  • Deployment of 20 UC CHTM students who qualified for J1 Cultural Exchange Program to the US.

2019

  • Strengthen community partnerships through Project UC HELEN with the Municipality of Tublay.

2020

  • The college initiated monitoring and extended assistance such as food packs to stranded students within Baguio City until they were safely home and was also one of the partners of Green Pepper Dos Restaurant for their Food for the Frontliners Program for 35 days with 3, 226 beneficiaries.
